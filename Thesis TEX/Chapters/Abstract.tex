\begin{abstract}
  A comparison of several meta-learning strategies in order to ascertain the
  truth of the no free lunch theorem as it applies to metalearning strategies
  was performed. The data needed in order to validate the performance of each
  meta-learning strategy was gathered from the UCI Irvine dataset repository,
  parsed, then stored in a database. Collections of datasets are then created
  within this database and the meta-learning strategies are compared in terms
  of their accuracies with relation to these collections. $t$ score analysis is
  then performed in order to determine whether the relative accuracies of the
  meta-learning algorithms are within expectation.

\end{abstract}
